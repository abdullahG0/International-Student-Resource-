FAQ

Got questions? We’ve got instant answers — updated November 2025.

Visa & Immigration

Do I need a visa to study in Scotland?

Yes — almost all non-UK/Irish students need a Student visa (formerly Tier 4). You apply after receiving your CAS from your university.

How much bank balance do I need to show?

£1,334 per month for living in Edinburgh/Glasgow (max 9 months = £12,006) + unpaid tuition fees. Money must be held for 28 consecutive days.

Can my family join me?

Only if your course is postgraduate (master’s/PhD) and lasts 9+ months. Dependants get full work rights.

Accommodation

When should I book university halls?

Apply the day you get your unconditional offer — best rooms disappear in 24–48 hours.

Is it safe to pay deposit before seeing the flat?

Never pay before video call + contract. Use our 10-point scam checklist in the Accommodation guide.

Healthcare & NHS

Do I have to pay the Immigration Health Surcharge (IHS)?

Yes — £776 per year (2025 rate). This gives you full free NHS access (GP, hospital, prescriptions in Scotland).

How do I register with a GP?

Bring BRP + proof of address (tenancy agreement or university letter). Takes 10 minutes.

Jobs & Money

How many hours can I work?

20 hours/week during term time, full-time during holidays.

Can I open a UK bank account as a student?

Yes — bring BRP + university letter. Monzo, Starling, HSBC, and Santander are fastest (same-day accounts).

General Scotland Life

Is Scotland colder than England?

Yes — pack warm waterproof jacket, gloves, and hat. Edinburgh winter averages 2–7°C.

Under-22s get free bus travel — is it true?

100% true! Apply for your Young Scot NEC card with your BRP and travel free on all Scottish buses.
